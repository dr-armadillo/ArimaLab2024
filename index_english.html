Enzyme Chemistry for People's Lives

　Enzymes are proteins that catalyze chemical reactions necessary for all living organisms to survive. Their function is necessary to sustain the life of all living organisms, and people have long used enzymes unknowingly in the form of "fermentation". A broad look at our surroundings reveals a variety of enzymes and microorganisms that are useful in industry and daily life.
　The Arima Laboratory is working on finding useful enzymes through microbial fermentation and applying them to various fields such as effective utilization of unused resources and manufacturing.

Greetings

Welcome to the web site of Arima Laboratory. In our laboratory, we are conducting research based on ideas for applications based on enzyme functions, and on technologies and strategies to enhance the usefulness of enzymes and microorganisms. Students are encouraged to pursue their own original research themes once they belong to our laboratory, but we encourage them to reach beyond the boundaries of their own themes to explore anything they are interested in and deepen the breadth of their own techniques and perspectives. In recent years, we have begun research on Sakaiminato salmon aquaculture and the effective use of crab shells, which are produced in large quantities in Tottori Prefecture. We hope to develop a living knowledge by combining not only the information obtained in classroom lectures but also the actual experience gained in the field. If you are at all interested in our research themes, please contact us at arima@tottori-u.ac.jp. We look forward to hearing from you.

Research contents

In the Arima Laboratory, we are researching the search for useful microorganisms from the natural world, functional analysis, and structural analysis of proteins and enzymes to add value to various goods and phenomena using the full range of protein, enzyme, and microorganism functions. In the search for useful microorganisms, we also aim to conduct glocal research combining issues specific to the fisheries industry and themes of global thinking to meet the demands of Tottori Prefecture.

Bacteriology

Actinomycetes and aquaponics flora: For crab shell waste utilization, we aim to acquire bacteria that have the potential to degrading chitin. Aquaponics: We aim to use the acquired low-temperature nitrifying bacterial flora for the aquaculture of silver salmon and other low-temperature-reared fish.

Protein Chemistry

The goal of protein chemistry research: To analyze the structure of theenzymes and proteins and to reconstitute them into proteins with better functionality. So far, we have conducted research on the conversion of peptide-degrading enzymes to synthetic enzymes.

Material function

New functions in unused resources and enzyme reaction products The target of unutilized resources is fishery waste, which is expected to yield new functional substances, and enzyme reaction products which leads to the discovery of new functions for known substances.
