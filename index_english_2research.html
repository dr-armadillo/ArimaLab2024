The "great power" of living things and materials invisible to the human eye

History Proves Accelerated Progress in Future Microbiotechnologies
　In 1665, Dr. Löwenhoek of the Netherlands invented the microscope, which made it possible to see smaller objects.　Then, in 1674, "bacteria" were discovered, and beyond that, science and technology related to biotechnology developed at an accelerated pace.　This was followed by the discovery by Dr. Pasteur (1862) that disproved the theory of spontaneous generation of microorganisms.　After that, the foundations of microbiology and enzymology have been laid, including the establishment of the concept of enzymes by Dr. Jokichi Takamine and the proof that alcoholic fermentation is possible with yeast extracts by Dr. Buchner.　These discoveries, which make visible what the eye cannot see, have contributed greatly to the accelerated progress of science and technology.　Today, AI has come into the world and is able to predict phenomena and even see the movement of molecules.　In the future, we can expect even more accelerated biotechnological progress through the fusion of AI and genome big data.

Fascination with Microbial and Enzymatic Functions
　Now, despite these developments in biotechnology and enzymology / microbiology science, 99% of the microorganisms in the world remain unclarified.　The reason for this was simple: the "inability to culture" was the reason for not being able to obtain information.　In recent years, environmental genome analysis has made it possible to cover the ecosystem of microorganisms.　However, it has not yet been possible to obtain information on individual genes.　With the integration of AI, this kind of information acquisition will become a reality in the not too distant future.
　On the other hand, since wet experiments are essential to prove facts, it will take decades to finalize genomic information + AI predictions.　It is easy to imagine that even with microorganisms that can be cultured, some ingenuity and wisdom will be required to link their original functions to genomic information.　Based on this background, our laboratory focuses on both microorganisms that cannot be cultured and microorganisms that can be cultured, focusing on "enzymes" that are active in their cells, with a view to making the most of their functions.　The elucidation of these new metabolic functions of microorganisms will lead to the provision of new information to the world.　We are working on the following project and would be happy to hear from you if you are interested.

Bacteriology

The main focus is on actinomycetes and aquaponics flora. Actinomycetes: Focusing on metabolism of waste and secondary metabolism. We aim to acquire bacteria with potential to produce useful substances. Aquaponics: We aim to use the acquired low-temperature nitrifying bacterial flora

Protein Chemistry

The goal of protein chemistry research is to analyze the structure of the enzymes, and to reconstitute into enzymes with superior functionality. So far, we have conducted research on the conversion of peptidases to peptide ligases and on the strengthening of chitin bonds in chitin-degrading enzymes.

Material function

We are searching for new functions in unused resources and enzyme reaction products. The target of unutilized resources is Tottori Prefecture fishery waste. On the other hand, the structure of enzyme reaction products is predictable, leading to the discovery of new functions for known substances.
